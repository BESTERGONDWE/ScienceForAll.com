1. Limited Treatment Options; Microbial resistance can lead to limited treatment options for infections, as antimicrobial-resistant infections may require the use of second- and third-line treatments, which can be less effective and cause serious side effects .

2. Increased Morbidity and Mortality: Drug-resistant infections can result in increased morbidity and mortality, as they can be harder to treat and may lead to more severe illness or complications.

3. Harmful Side Effects: The use of second- and third-line treatments for antimicrobial-resistant infections can harm patients by causing serious side effects.

4. Public Health Impact: Antimicrobial resistance can have a significant public health impact, as it may lead to the spread of drug-resistant strains of bacteria, making it more challenging to control and treat infections.

5. Overuse of Antibiotics: Overuse of antibiotics can promote antibiotic resistance, leading to the development of resistant germs and causing needless and harmful side effects.

These effects highlight the importance of addressing antimicrobial resistance to ensure effective treatment options and minimize the public health impact of drug-resistant infections.
